    Our History




    The two competing national suffrage organizations before us were the NWSA and AWSA. They lasted over two decades. Suffragists worked to mend the split from the start, but were unsuccessful. Alice Stone Blackwell, the daughter of the American association leader Lucy Stone, spearheaded successful negotiations to merge the two groups in 1890.

    After the combination of the two groups, the NWSA and AWSA to create this organization, NAWSA, we quickly got to work.

    Our goal as an organization is to convince not only the men and women around us that it is our obligation to fight for women's suffrage, but we need to also convince those of higher power to create the change that we are pushing for, which is the right for women to vote in this democratic nation

    Our Beliefs




    As pushers for equality we believe in one idea and it is very simple

    Men and women are equal. The ideals we stand for in NAWSA aren’t solely to put women on a pedestal higher than men, it is to raise women to the same level that men are at. This would mean equal treatment for both men and women. This starts with the most prominent right that men have but not women. The right to vote. This leads into the overall goals of our organization, NAWSA

    Our Goal




    Our goal as an organization is to:

    Convince not only the men and women around us that it is our obligation to fight for women's suffrage, but we need to also convince those of higher power to create the change that we are pushing for, which is the right for women to vote in this democratic nation

    Who We Want





    If you agree with the idea that women should have the right to vote in this country, I, along with Carrie Chapman Catt the president of the NAWSA organization, personally welcome you to join us and the rest of the NAWSA organization! If you stand for this right you have a place to fight alongside us in this battle for women’s suffrage.

    Methodology





    Protests are a big portion of what we do here at NAWSA. We believe that it is an effective way to spread the idea of women suffrage to the majority of the public and even high ranking officials who will hear of our protests through various sources.

    Future Plans





    Our Plan

    In Washington DC, we plan to have a parade in which you can join us. We will march through the streets of Washington in hopes to get the attention of the White House and invoke change in our favor.
